Best things to do in Chefchaouen, the Blue City of Morocco

Awash with blue paint, the Chefchaouen Medina is the maze-like historical heart of this Moroccan city beneath the Rif Mountains. Alleyways, staircases, and small squares allow visitors to get lost in the beautiful, distinctive neighborhood, while also serving as vivid photo backdrops thanks to their unique aesthetic. The extravagant appearance of the medina can make it hard to remember that this remains a residential area.

Chefchaouen medina.

Among a sea of blue houses in the medina of Chefchaouen, the city’s Kasbah stands out with its high clay walls. Inside those walls, you’ll find a lush garden, fountains, and towers with prime city views, as well as an ethnographic museum that details the customs and interests of Chefchaouen’s people through items such as costumes, instruments, and weapons.

Kasbah Museum

The whole valley in northern Morocco looks like a secret garden, with high mountains and lush vegetation along the banks of the river that feeds Akchour Falls. The falls consist of a high waterfall up in the valley, a series of small cascades, and a more modest waterfall below, all accessed by hiking trails.
